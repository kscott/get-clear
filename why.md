Reminders, Calendar, Contacts, Mail, Messages. I'm trying to name the group.

There's a list of words. Accord (too Honda). Pact (too governmental). Thread, Keep, Poise. None of them fit.

Then the frame shifts.

It's the people. The ones I'm working with. And the promises I've made. I'm not done yet.

**Get Clear**.

## The moments

**The follow-up you almost forgot**

You finish a call. Sarah mentioned she'd send something by Friday. You're still in the conversation — you want to stay there.

> *"Remind me to follow up with Sarah if I haven't heard from her by Friday afternoon"*

```text
reminders add "Follow up with Sarah" friday 3pm note "if she hasn't responded"
```

```text
Added: Follow up with Sarah (in Work) · due Friday 3:00pm + note
```

Nothing slipped. You never left the room.

**The meeting you just promised**

You promised a meeting. Marcus is counting on you.

> *"Add a 30-minute check-in with Marcus next Tuesday at 2pm"*

```text
calendar add "Check-in with Marcus" tuesday 2pm to 2:30pm
```

```text
Added: Check-in with Marcus · Tuesday March 31 · 2:00–2:30pm
```

You kept your word in under ten seconds.

## The interface disappears

You never think about the tool. You think about what needs to happen, and it's handled. You never leave the room you were already in.

You say what needs to happen in plain language. The meeting gets added. The email goes out. The reminder is set. You stay present — in the conversation, in the work, with the people.

The conversation is your safety net. *"Oops, that wasn't right"* is a complete recovery instruction. No pop-ups asking if you're sure. No confirmation screens. Just the relationship, and the work, and the trust it's been handled correctly.

---

### Two ways to use it

**With Claude** — tell Claude what you need in plain language. It handles the commands. Less to memorize, more conversational.

**Directly** — use the vocabulary, type the commands. Fast, precise, no dependency on anything except your Mac. `reminders add "Call Sarah" friday priority high` — readable enough to type from memory.

Six words, five tools: `add`, `find`, `show`, `change`, `rename`, `remove`. Use one, know them all.

Both ways are first-class. The tools work without Claude. Claude makes them faster and easier — but the tools stand on their own.

## Commitments kept

Doing the things is half the story. Knowing you did them is the other half.

`what` shows you everything that happened — reminders added, emails sent, events created. `recap` goes further: just the commitments kept. The promises you made and honored.

At the end of a hard day you want to look back and say to yourself — I did the things. I kept my word. I was the person I'm trying to be. That's not a report. That's affirmation. That's what lets you close the laptop and actually be present.

That's not productivity. That's integrity. That's the person you are.
